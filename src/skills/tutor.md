# {{SKILL}}: tutor

> {{DESC_TUTOR}}

**Trigger:** the user wants to *learn* something fast rather than have it done for them — "teach me X", "get me functional in X", "drill me on X until it sticks", or "this is confusing, help me understand it". This is teaching the user a skill or concept; for gathering and verifying facts from the web use [research](research.md), and for converting a document to read it use [ingest](ingest.md).

## Procedure
1. Pin the target and pick the mode. Establish what they want to walk away able to do, and how much time and prior footing they have. Choose: **crash course** (go from zero to functional), **error drill** (turn shaky knowledge into reflex), or **decode** (make confusing material click). If it's unclear which, ask once, then proceed.
2. **Crash course** — act as a teacher who has only this one session and will never see them again; the sole objective is to make them *functional* before time runs out. Name what to learn first, what to ignore completely, and the single exercise that, done once, puts them ahead of most people who have studied this for months. No theory without immediate practical use; no bare lists.
3. **Error drill** — do not explain the concept. Drop them straight into a realistic situation where they'd have to use it and would probably slip. When they make a mistake, withhold the answer and instead ask the one question that forces them to find where their reasoning broke. Reveal the answer only after at least two honest attempts. Repeat the cycle until they get it right without hesitation.
4. **Decode** — before explaining anything, find the one sentence that, once understood, makes the rest fall into place. Explain only that sentence first, using an everyday analogy with no technical terms. Then ask three questions only someone who truly understood it could answer; do not move on until they pass all three.
5. Close by naming the next thing to practise and the gap most likely to trip them, so the session continues without you.

## Done when
- The user can do or explain the target unprompted — functional, reflexive, or having passed the comprehension check — and knows the next thing to practise.
